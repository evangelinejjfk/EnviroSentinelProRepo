**"Now the tutorial is focusing on the alert counters at the top of the screen. These are incredibly important.**

**In our Sacramento flood scenario, you can see we currently have:**

**"The tutorial is now pointing to the location selector in the header. This is how you customize EnviroSentinel Pro for your specific area.**

**The moment you change locations, all the data refreshes - alerts, forecasts, maps, everything. This means first responders can quickly switch between different affected areas during a regional emergency.**

## STEP 6: Flood Forecasting Module

**"Excellent! We've navigated to the Floods page. This is where the real power of EnviroSentinel Pro shines for flood-prone areas like Sacramento.**

**What you're seeing here is a comprehensive flood monitoring system that combines:**
- Real-time precipitation data
- River gauge levels from USGS sensors
- Terrain and watershed analysis
- Historical flood patterns
- Machine learning predictions

**On the map, you can see:**
- The Sacramento River running through the city
- Color-coded flood risk zones - green is low risk, yellow is moderate, orange is high, and red is critical
- Active river gauges showing current water levels
- Predicted flood extent for the next 24-48 hours

**In our demo scenario, you can see the American River and Sacramento River are both showing elevated levels. The system is predicting potential overflow in low-lying areas near I Street.**

**Studies show that early flood warnings like this can reduce property damage by 30-40% and give families crucial time to evacuate safely. Let's see what other threats we're monitoring."**

---

## STEP 7: Wildfire Detection

**"Now we're on the Wildfires page. While we're focused on floods in Sacramento, EnviroSentinel Pro monitors all environmental threats simultaneously - because these threats often overlap.**

**The wildfire detection system uses:**
- Satellite thermal imaging
- Ground sensor networks
- Smoke detection cameras
- Weather patterns and wind data
- Fuel moisture content

**Even in our Sacramento flood scenario, there's a small wildfire alert in the foothills east of the city. This is actually realistic - California can experience both floods and fires in the same season.**

**The map shows:**
- Active fire perimeters in red
- Fire danger ratings by zone
- Smoke dispersion patterns
- Evacuation zones
- Safe corridors for escape

**For communities in the wildland-urban interface, this information is literally life-saving. Wildfires can spread at speeds up to 14 miles per hour in grasslands. Minutes matter. Moving on."**

---

## STEP 8: Heat Island Monitoring

**"We've moved to the Heat monitoring module. This page tracks heat waves and urban heat islands - those areas in cities where temperatures are significantly higher than surrounding rural areas.**

**The heat map shows temperature variations across Sacramento. Notice how:**
- Downtown areas with lots of concrete and asphalt show higher temperatures
- Parks and green spaces appear cooler
- The Sacramento River corridor provides natural cooling
- Industrial areas retain more heat

**This matters because:**
- Heat-related deaths have increased 60% in recent decades
- Vulnerable populations like the elderly and homeless are most at risk
- Urban heat islands can be 15-20 degrees hotter than nearby rural areas

**City planners use this data to:**
- Place cooling centers strategically
- Design green infrastructure projects
- Target tree planting initiatives
- Protect at-risk communities

**Let's check out the community features next."**

---

## STEP 9: Community Reports

**"Here's the Community page - one of my favorite features. This is where crowdsourcing meets environmental monitoring.**

**What makes EnviroSentinel Pro special is that it combines official data from satellites and sensors with real-time observations from people on the ground.**

**Community members can submit reports about:**
- Rising water levels they're observing
- Smoke they see or smell
- Road flooding or closures
- Downed power lines
- Animals in distress
- Evacuations in progress

**On the map, you can see report markers. Each marker shows:**
- What was reported
- When it was submitted
- Verification status
- Photos or videos if available
- Confidence score based on multiple reports

**In our Sacramento flood scenario, you might see residents reporting:**
- 'Water is over the curb on J Street'
- 'Yolo Bypass is completely flooded'
- 'Saw sandbags being distributed at the community center'

**This local knowledge fills critical gaps in official data. A satellite might not see flooding under tree cover, but a resident will. Your observation could save your neighbor's life. Let's look at personal impact tracking."**
